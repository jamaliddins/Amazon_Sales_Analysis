Key Insights
1. Order Quantity vs Sales Amount (B2B vs B2C)

A statistically significant relationship exists between order quantity and sales amount (Spearman correlation, p < 0.05).
While B2C dominates in transaction volume, median sales values are similar across both segments, indicating comparable per-order value.

2. Revenue per Unit (B2B vs B2C)

B2B orders generate significantly higher revenue per unit compared to B2C.
The Mann-Whitney U test confirms this difference (p < 0.05), with an approximate +7% uplift.

3. Sales by Product Category

Sales amounts differ significantly across product categories (Kruskal-Wallis, p < 0.05).
Certain categories (e.g., Ethnic Dress) show higher median sales, while others consistently underperform, indicating category-driven revenue variation.

4. Revenue Variability by Order Size

Revenue variability differs significantly across order size groups (Levene’s test, p < 0.05).
Smaller grouped orders (2–5 items) show higher variability compared to single-item orders, suggesting inconsistent purchasing behavior.

5. Cancellation Behavior & Fulfilment Method

Cancellation rates are significantly associated with fulfilment method (Chi-square, p < 0.05).
Orders fulfilled by Amazon show higher cancellation concentration, indicating potential operational or customer experience issues.
